# Myasthenia Gravis — Wiki Home

Welcome to this wiki on Myasthenia Gravis (MG). Whether you have just been diagnosed, are a carer supporting someone with MG, or are simply looking to understand the condition more deeply, this resource aims to give you clear, honest, and up-to-date information.

MG is a manageable — though not yet curable — condition. With the right treatment and the right knowledge, the majority of people with MG can lead fulfilling lives. As one patient puts it: **"I have myasthenia, it doesn't have me."**

---

## Quick Reference

| At a glance | Detail |
|---|---|
| What is it? | A chronic autoimmune disease that causes fatigable muscle weakness |
| How common? | Approximately 20 per 100,000 people; prevalence rising |
| Who is affected? | Anyone — most common in women under 40 and men over 60 |
| Main symptoms | Drooping eyelids, double vision, difficulty speaking, swallowing, and breathing; limb weakness |
| Key feature | Weakness worsens with activity and improves with rest (fatiguability) |
| Cause | Immune system attacks the neuromuscular junction (the gap between nerve and muscle) |
| Is it curable? | Not yet, but highly treatable — most people achieve good control |
| Is it inherited? | Not directly, though genetic susceptibility plays a role |
| Is it contagious? | No |
| Life expectancy | Normal for most people with MG |

## About this wiki

This wiki was compiled from multiple sources including the NHS, NINDS, Myaware, MDA, and peer-reviewed medical literature (including the *Journal of Clinical Investigation*). It is intended to support informed conversations with healthcare professionals — not to replace individual medical advice. MG is a complex and rapidly evolving field; always discuss treatment decisions with a specialist neurologist experienced in neuromuscular disease.
